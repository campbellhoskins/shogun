## 1. PURPOSE AND LEGAL CONTEXT

### 1.1 Purpose

This Exhibit defines (a) the Duty of Care services Direct Travel will provide to support Client's travel risk management ("**TRM**") program, (b) Severity Level classifications and corresponding service level objectives, (c) roles and responsibilities of each party, (d) data handling and privacy requirements, (e) escalation and crisis communication protocols, and (f) limitations applicable to such services.

### 1.2 Regulatory Background

The services described herein are designed to assist Client in meeting its obligations as an employer under applicable health and safety, employment, and common-law duty of care frameworks, which may include:

- **United States** — OSHA General Duty Clause, Section 5(a)(1) of the Occupational Safety and Health Act of 1970, requiring employers to furnish a place of employment free from recognized hazards causing or likely to cause death or serious physical harm;
- **United Kingdom** — Health and Safety at Work Act 1974, requiring employers to ensure employees' health, safety, and welfare so far as is reasonably practicable; the Management of Health and Safety at Work Regulations 1999, requiring risk assessments and relevant employee information and training; and the Corporate Manslaughter and Corporate Homicide Act 2007;
- **European Union** — Framework Directive 89/391/EEC, obligating employers to ensure the safety and health of workers in every aspect related to work, including risk assessment and preventive measures;
- **International Standard** — ISO 31030:2021 (Travel Risk Management), the first international standard for travel risk management, derived from ISO 31000, providing a structured framework covering policy development, threat identification, risk assessment, prevention and mitigation strategies, traveler tracking, two-way communication, crisis management planning, and programme evaluation.

Client acknowledges that Client retains **primary responsibility** for its duty of care obligations as an employer. Direct Travel provides services to enable and support Client's TRM program as specified herein and does not assume the employer's duty of care. Nothing in this Exhibit transfers, delegates, or otherwise alters the allocation of Client's duty of care obligations at law.

### 1.3 Standard of Care

Direct Travel will perform the services described in this Exhibit using **commercially reasonable efforts** consistent with generally accepted practices in the travel management industry for organizations of similar size, scope, and risk profile. The standard is one of reasonableness, not perfection.

## 2. DEFINITIONS

**2.1** "**Approved Channels**" means bookings made through (a) Client's designated online booking tool(s) as provided by Direct Travel, (b) Direct Travel agent-assisted channels (phone, email, online chat), and (c) any other booking channels expressly approved in writing by Client and integrated into the TMC's itinerary data feed.

**2.2** "**After-Hours**" means times outside Business Hours, as defined in the SOW, including applicable time zone(s). Direct Travel maintains follow-the-sun global service teams providing 24/7/365 coverage.

**2.3** "**Business Hours**" means the hours defined in the SOW during which Client's primary service team is staffed, including applicable time zone(s).

**2.4** "**Crisis Bridge**" means a real-time conference line and/or digital collaboration channel established during Level 4 Incidents to coordinate response actions between Direct Travel, Client, and, where applicable, Specialist Providers.

**2.5** "**Duty of Care Services**" means the services described in Section 3.

**2.6** "**Risk Intelligence Platform**" means the third-party risk management platform designated by Direct Travel (currently Crisis24 Horizon) providing global threat monitoring, traveler locate capabilities, and mass communication, as may be updated from time to time upon reasonable notice to Client.

**2.7** "**Incident**" means an event that may reasonably impact Traveler safety, security, health, or ability to continue travel, including without limitation: natural disasters and severe weather events; civil unrest, political instability, and terrorism or security events; public health emergencies, disease outbreaks, and pandemics; major transportation disruptions (airport closures, airline groundings, rail strikes); infrastructure failures; or other significant events as determined by Direct Travel using commercially reasonable judgment.

**2.8** "**Off-Channel Booking**" means any travel booking made by or on behalf of a Traveler outside of Approved Channels, including direct bookings on consumer travel sites, airline or hotel websites, or personal travel agency arrangements.

**2.9** "**PNR**" means Passenger Name Record, the foundational booking data record containing passenger name, itinerary segments, ticket information, contact details, and associated booking elements maintained in the global distribution system or NDC connection.

**2.10** "**Severity Level**" means the Level 1 through Level 4 classification described in Section 5.

**2.11** "**Specialist Provider**" means a third-party provider of security, medical, insurance, evacuation, or other specialized risk management services engaged by Client or by Direct Travel on Client's behalf, including without limitation Crisis24 (a GardaWorld company), International SOS, or other providers specified in the SOW.

**2.12** "**Traveler**" means a Client employee or other person authorized by Client (including but not limited to contractors, consultants, candidates, board members, and guests) traveling on Client business whose itinerary is booked through Approved Channels or otherwise provided to Direct Travel for visibility.

**2.13** "**Traveler Data**" means personal data related to Travelers processed by Direct Travel in connection with providing services under the Agreement, including itinerary details, contact information, profile data, location information, and welfare check responses.

**2.14** "**Platform**" means Direct Travel's integrated technology ecosystem, including the designated online booking tool, Risk Intelligence Platform integration, and associated risk management, reporting, and communication tools, as may be updated from time to time.
