# Meridian Global Solutions — Corporate Travel Duty of Care Policy

**Policy ID:** MGS-DOC-2025-001
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025
**Last Revised:** November 15, 2024
**Applies To:** All employees, contractors, and authorized travelers conducting business on behalf of Meridian Global Solutions ("the Company")

---

## 1. Purpose and Scope

This policy establishes the Company's obligations and procedures for protecting the health, safety, and security of all personnel traveling for business purposes. It applies to all domestic and international travel arranged through or on behalf of the Company, regardless of trip duration or destination.

The Company has a legal and moral duty of care to take all reasonable steps to ensure traveler safety before, during, and after business travel. Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action and may expose the Company to legal liability.

---

## 2. Destination Risk Classification

All destinations are classified using a four-tier risk system aligned with U.S. Department of State Travel Advisories and supplemented by internal security assessments.

### Risk Level 1 — Standard (Exercise Normal Precautions)
Countries with stable political environments, low crime rates, and adequate medical infrastructure. Examples: Canada, United Kingdom, Japan, Australia, Germany.

### Risk Level 2 — Elevated (Exercise Increased Caution)
Countries with moderate security concerns, periodic civil unrest, or limited medical access in certain regions. Examples: Mexico, India, Turkey, South Africa, Brazil.

### Risk Level 3 — High (Reconsider Travel)
Countries with significant security threats, active conflict zones in certain regions, or severe health risks. Examples: Pakistan, Nigeria, Colombia (certain departments), Kenya (border regions).

### Risk Level 4 — Critical (Do Not Travel)
Countries with active armed conflict, widespread terrorism, government collapse, or ongoing epidemic with no available medical countermeasures. Examples: Syria, Yemen, Somalia, South Sudan, Afghanistan.

**Risk levels are reviewed quarterly by the Security Operations team and updated immediately when the State Department issues advisory changes.**

---

## 3. Travel Approval Requirements

### Level 1 Destinations
- Booking may proceed through standard channels without additional approval.
- Traveler must register the trip in the Company travel portal.

### Level 2 Destinations
- Requires **direct manager approval** prior to booking.
- Traveler must complete the Pre-Travel Risk Acknowledgment Form.
- Travel Security team will issue a destination briefing within 48 hours of approval.

### Level 3 Destinations
- Requires approval from **both the direct manager and the VP of the traveler's division**.
- Mandatory pre-travel security briefing conducted by the Security Operations team.
- Traveler must carry a satellite communication device issued by the Company.
- A designated in-country security liaison must be arranged prior to departure.

### Level 4 Destinations
- **All travel to Level 4 destinations is prohibited** unless an exception is granted by the Chief Security Officer (CSO) and the CEO jointly.
- Exceptions require a written business justification, a detailed security plan, and executive-level risk acceptance.
- If approved, the traveler must be accompanied by a professional security escort at all times.

## 4. Insurance and Medical Requirements

### Minimum Insurance Coverage

All business travelers must be covered under the Company's corporate travel insurance policy. Coverage minimums by destination risk level:

| Risk Level | Medical Emergency | Medical Evacuation | Repatriation of Remains | Trip Cancellation |
|------------|------------------|--------------------|------------------------|-------------------|
| Level 1    | $250,000         | $100,000           | Included               | Included          |
| Level 2    | $500,000         | $250,000           | Included               | Included          |
| Level 3    | $1,000,000       | $500,000           | Included               | Included          |
| Level 4    | $2,000,000       | $1,000,000         | Included               | Included          |

### Vaccination Requirements

Travelers must comply with all destination-specific vaccination requirements as determined by the CDC and the World Health Organization. Required vaccinations must be completed **no fewer than 14 days** before departure.

- **All international travel:** COVID-19 vaccination (up to date), Routine vaccinations (MMR, Tetanus, Influenza)
- **Sub-Saharan Africa, South America (tropical regions):** Yellow Fever (International Certificate of Vaccination required at entry), Typhoid, Hepatitis A and B, Malaria prophylaxis
- **South and Southeast Asia:** Typhoid, Hepatitis A and B, Japanese Encephalitis (for rural travel exceeding 30 days), Rabies (if working with animals or in remote areas)
- **Middle East and North Africa:** Hepatitis A and B, Meningococcal vaccine (required for Saudi Arabia during Hajj/Umrah seasons)

Travelers with medical conditions that prevent vaccination must obtain a written exemption from the Company Medical Officer and may be denied travel to certain destinations.

### Pre-Travel Medical Clearance

Travelers to Level 3 and Level 4 destinations must obtain medical clearance from the Company Medical Officer no more than 30 days before departure. This includes verification of vaccinations, assessment of fitness to travel, and provisioning of any necessary prescription medications.

## 5. Emergency Contact and Communication Protocol

### Emergency Contact Hierarchy

In the event of an emergency during business travel, the following escalation chain applies:

1. **Immediate:** Contact the 24/7 Travel Emergency Hotline at +1-800-555-0199 (operated by Global Rescue, the Company's emergency assistance provider)
2. **Within 1 hour:** The Travel Emergency Hotline notifies the Company's **Travel Risk Manager** (currently: Diana Vasquez, diana.vasquez@meridianglobal.com)
3. **Within 2 hours:** The Travel Risk Manager notifies the traveler's **direct manager** and the **VP of Security Operations** (currently: Marcus Chen, marcus.chen@meridianglobal.com)
4. **Within 4 hours (for serious incidents):** The VP of Security Operations convenes the **Crisis Management Team**, which includes the CSO, General Counsel, VP of HR, and relevant division leadership.

### Communication Requirements

- **Level 1 destinations:** No mandatory check-ins. Traveler should be reachable via standard company communication channels.
- **Level 2 destinations:** Traveler must check in with their direct manager **every 48 hours** via email or phone.
- **Level 3 destinations:** Traveler must check in with the Travel Risk Manager **every 24 hours** via the Company's secure communication app or satellite phone.
- **Level 4 destinations (if exception granted):** Traveler must check in with the Security Operations Center **every 12 hours** and must have active GPS tracking enabled on the Company-issued device at all times.

### Traveler Location Tracking

The Company uses its travel management platform to maintain real-time visibility of all active business travelers. Travelers are required to book through approved channels to ensure their itineraries are captured in the tracking system. Travelers who book outside approved channels and cannot be located during an emergency may be subject to disciplinary action.

## 6. Approved Vendors and Booking Procedures

### Airlines
- **Preferred carriers (domestic):** United Airlines, Delta Air Lines, American Airlines
- **Preferred carriers (international):** United Airlines, Delta Air Lines, British Airways, Lufthansa, Singapore Airlines
- Non-preferred carriers may be booked only if preferred carriers do not offer service on the required route or if the fare difference exceeds **$500 round-trip**.

### Hotels
- Accommodations must meet minimum security standards: 24-hour front desk, electronic room locks, in-room safe, and fire safety certification.
- **Level 2+ destinations:** Hotels must be from the approved security-vetted list maintained by Security Operations.
- Ground-floor rooms are prohibited in Level 2+ destinations.

### Ground Transportation
- Company-approved car services or hotel-arranged transportation only in Level 2+ destinations.
- Ride-sharing services (Uber, Lyft) are permitted in Level 1 destinations only.
- Rental cars must include comprehensive insurance coverage. Drivers must hold a valid license recognized in the destination country.

### Booking Channel
All travel must be booked through the Company's authorized Travel Management Company (TMC) or the approved online booking tool. Direct bookings with suppliers are not permitted except in documented emergencies.

## 7. Traveler Responsibilities

All travelers are expected to:

1. **Register all trips** in the Company travel portal prior to departure.
2. **Review destination-specific safety information** provided by Security Operations.
3. **Carry emergency contact information** at all times, including the Travel Emergency Hotline number, local embassy contact, and local emergency services numbers.
4. **Comply with all local laws and customs** of the destination country.
5. **Report any safety or security concerns** immediately to the Travel Emergency Hotline.
6. **Maintain situational awareness** and avoid unnecessary risk-taking, including traveling to known dangerous areas, walking alone at night in unfamiliar locations, or publicly displaying expensive personal items.
7. **Comply with check-in requirements** as specified for the destination's risk level.
8. **Retain all travel documentation** (boarding passes, hotel receipts, incident reports) for 90 days after return.

## 8. Incident Response and Crisis Management

### Incident Classification

- **Category 1 (Minor):** Lost luggage, minor illness, missed connection, minor theft. Handled by the TMC and Travel Emergency Hotline.
- **Category 2 (Serious):** Hospitalization, assault, natural disaster affecting travel area, significant civil unrest. Triggers Travel Risk Manager notification and potential Crisis Management Team activation.
- **Category 3 (Critical):** Life-threatening injury or illness, kidnapping, active terrorist attack, political detention. Immediate Crisis Management Team activation, potential medical evacuation, and government liaison engagement.

### Evacuation Procedures

In the event that evacuation is necessary:
- The Crisis Management Team authorizes and coordinates all evacuations.
- **Medical evacuation:** Coordinated through Global Rescue. Air ambulance for Category 3 medical emergencies. Commercial flight evacuation for Category 2 if safe to travel.
- **Security evacuation:** Coordinated through the Company's contracted security firm (currently: Garrison Security International). Includes overland extraction to safe harbor and chartered air evacuation if commercial options are unavailable.
- **Cost:** All evacuation costs are borne by the Company. Travelers should not delay evacuation due to cost concerns.

### Post-Incident Requirements

After any Category 2 or Category 3 incident:
- Traveler must complete an **Incident Report** within 72 hours of the event.
- The Travel Risk Manager conducts a **post-incident review** within 14 days.
- The Company will provide access to **counseling and mental health support** through the Employee Assistance Program (EAP) for a minimum of 6 months following the incident.
- **Lessons learned** are documented and incorporated into policy updates as needed.

---

## 9. Policy Compliance and Enforcement

Compliance with this policy is mandatory. Violations may result in:
- Denial of travel expense reimbursement
- Restriction of future travel privileges
- Formal disciplinary action up to and including termination

The Travel Risk Manager conducts quarterly audits of travel bookings and compliance records. An annual policy review is conducted by the Security Operations team in coordination with Legal, HR, and executive leadership.
