## 1. PURPOSE AND LEGAL CONTEXT

### 1.1 Purpose

This Exhibit defines (a) Duty of Care services Direct Travel will provide to support Client's travel risk management ("**TRM**") program, (b) Severity Level classifications and service level objectives, (c) roles and responsibilities, (d) data handling and privacy requirements, and (e) limitations applicable to such services.

### 1.2 Regulatory Background

Services herein assist Client in meeting employer obligations under applicable duty of care frameworks, including:

- **United States** — OSHA General Duty Clause, Section 5(a)(1) of the Occupational Safety and Health Act of 1970;
- **United Kingdom** — Health and Safety at Work Act 1974; Management of Health and Safety at Work Regulations 1999; Corporate Manslaughter and Corporate Homicide Act 2007;
- **European Union** — Framework Directive 89/391/EEC;
- **International Standard** — ISO 31030:2021 (Travel Risk Management), derived from ISO 31000.

Client retains **primary responsibility** for its duty of care obligations as an employer. Direct Travel provides services to enable and support Client's TRM program and does not assume the employer's duty of care.

### 1.3 Standard of Care

Direct Travel will perform services using **commercially reasonable efforts** consistent with generally accepted practices in the travel management industry. The standard is one of reasonableness, not perfection.

## 2. SEVERITY LEVELS AND SERVICE LEVEL OBJECTIVES

### 2.1 Severity Level Definitions

| Level | Classification | Description | Examples |
|-------|---------------|-------------|----------|
| **Level 1** | Advisory | Minor disruption; informational alert only | Localized weather warning, minor transport delay |
| **Level 2** | Monitor | Moderate disruption; targeted alerts; optional welfare check | Regional transport strike, elevated security alert |
| **Level 3** | Action Required | Significant disruption; proactive outreach required | Major weather event in Traveler area, airport closure, disease outbreak |
| **Level 4** | Crisis | Immediate life safety risk; urgent outreach and crisis escalation | Terrorist attack, catastrophic natural disaster, pandemic border closures |

Severity determination is made by Direct Travel using commercially reasonable judgment informed by the Risk Intelligence Platform (currently Crisis24 Horizon).

### 2.2 Service Level Objectives

| Severity | Traveler Alert / Outreach | Client Escalation | Status Updates |
|----------|--------------------------|-------------------|----------------|
| **Level 1** | Advisory within 4 hours | Not required unless configured | As conditions change |
| **Level 2** | Targeted alert within 2 hours | Primary contact within 4 hours | Every 4 hours |
| **Level 3** | Outreach within 60 minutes | Escalation roster within 60 minutes | Every 2 hours |
| **Level 4** | Outreach within 30 minutes | Roster within 30 min; Crisis Bridge within 60 min | Every 30 min (acute); hourly (sustained) |

### 2.3 SLO Exceptions

SLOs may be impacted by: telecommunications outages; government-imposed communication restrictions; supplier system outages (GDS, airline, hotel); incomplete Traveler profile data; Off-Channel Bookings without itinerary data; simultaneous multi-region incidents; or force majeure events.

### 2.4 Traveler Outreach Standards

Welfare checks follow default priority: (1) SMS, (2) email, (3) push notification, (4) voice call. Travelers confirm status using:

| Response | Meaning | TMC Action |
|----------|---------|------------|
| **SAFE** | Not in danger; no assistance needed | Log status; no further action |
| **NEED ASSISTANCE** | Requires support | Attempt live contact within 15 min |
| **NOT IN AREA** | Not near affected area | Remove from active outreach |
| **No Response** | No reply within outreach window | Escalate to Client contacts |

## 3. TRAVELER DATA, PRIVACY, AND SECURITY

### 3.1 Data Roles

Client is the **data controller** for Traveler Data. Direct Travel acts as a **data processor**, processing Traveler Data solely on Client's documented instructions. Where required, a Data Processing Agreement ("**DPA**") is incorporated by reference.

### 3.2 Lawful Basis

Processing is supported by Client's **legitimate interest** in protecting health and safety of travelers under Article 6(1)(f) GDPR and equivalent laws. Client is responsible for any required Legitimate Interest Assessment or Data Protection Impact Assessment.

### 3.3 Minimum Required Traveler Profile Data

| Data Element | Purpose | Impact if Missing |
|-------------|---------|-------------------|
| **Full legal name** | Traveler identification, supplier matching | Cannot confirm identity |
| **Mobile phone number** (SMS-capable) | Primary welfare check channel | SMS outreach unavailable |
| **Email address** | Secondary communication channel | Email alerts unavailable |
| **Emergency contact** (name, relationship, phone) | Next-of-kin notification | Client must provide during incident |
| **Passport details** (international travel) | Documentation verification | Cannot verify travel documents |

Service effectiveness is materially reduced where profile data is incomplete.

### 3.4 Data Sharing During Incidents

Direct Travel may share **minimum necessary** Traveler Data with: (a) Client's escalation contacts; (b) travel suppliers for rebooking; (c) Specialist Providers (security, medical, evacuation) authorized in the SOW; and (d) government authorities where required by law or to protect life safety.

### 3.5 Location Data

Traveler location derived from itinerary records (PNR-based) reflects **planned** location and does not constitute real-time GPS tracking. Continuous GPS tracking requires explicit SOW inclusion, a Data Protection Impact Assessment, and documented Client authorization.

### 3.6 Cross-Border Transfers

Transfers outside the EEA, UK, or other restricted jurisdictions are subject to appropriate safeguards (e.g., Standard Contractual Clauses, adequacy decisions) as specified in the DPA.
