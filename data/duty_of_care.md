## 1. PURPOSE AND LEGAL CONTEXT

### 1.1 Purpose

This Exhibit defines (a) the Duty of Care services Direct Travel will provide to support Client's travel risk management ("**TRM**") program, (b) Severity Level classifications and corresponding service level objectives, (c) roles and responsibilities of each party, (d) data handling and privacy requirements, (e) escalation and crisis communication protocols, and (f) limitations applicable to such services.

### 1.2 Regulatory Background

The services described herein are designed to assist Client in meeting its obligations as an employer under applicable health and safety, employment, and common-law duty of care frameworks, which may include:

- **United States** — OSHA General Duty Clause, Section 5(a)(1) of the Occupational Safety and Health Act of 1970, requiring employers to furnish a place of employment free from recognized hazards causing or likely to cause death or serious physical harm;
- **United Kingdom** — Health and Safety at Work Act 1974, requiring employers to ensure employees' health, safety, and welfare so far as is reasonably practicable; the Management of Health and Safety at Work Regulations 1999, requiring risk assessments and relevant employee information and training; and the Corporate Manslaughter and Corporate Homicide Act 2007;
- **European Union** — Framework Directive 89/391/EEC, obligating employers to ensure the safety and health of workers in every aspect related to work, including risk assessment and preventive measures;
- **International Standard** — ISO 31030:2021 (Travel Risk Management), the first international standard for travel risk management, derived from ISO 31000, providing a structured framework covering policy development, threat identification, risk assessment, prevention and mitigation strategies, traveler tracking, two-way communication, crisis management planning, and programme evaluation.

Client acknowledges that Client retains **primary responsibility** for its duty of care obligations as an employer. Direct Travel provides services to enable and support Client's TRM program as specified herein and does not assume the employer's duty of care. Nothing in this Exhibit transfers, delegates, or otherwise alters the allocation of Client's duty of care obligations at law.

### 1.3 Standard of Care

Direct Travel will perform the services described in this Exhibit using **commercially reasonable efforts** consistent with generally accepted practices in the travel management industry for organizations of similar size, scope, and risk profile. The standard is one of reasonableness, not perfection.

## 3. SCOPE OF DUTY OF CARE SERVICES

### 3.1 Itinerary Visibility and Traveler Locate

**(a)** Direct Travel will capture and maintain itinerary records for all travel booked through Approved Channels, including air, hotel, rail, car rental, and ground transportation segments, regardless of airline distribution channel.

**(b)** Direct Travel will provide Client with access to reporting dashboards showing active Travelers by destination, travel dates, and booking status. Where included in the SOW, Direct Travel will provide access to an interactive global map displaying Traveler locations (based on itinerary data) overlaid with destination risk ratings and active threat alerts.

**(c)** When an Incident occurs, Direct Travel will, using available itinerary data, identify Travelers who are in or near the affected area ("**impact zone**") and make this information available to Client's designated contacts.

**(d)** Direct Travel will use commercially reasonable efforts to ingest itinerary data from Off-Channel Bookings where Client has deployed itinerary capture tools (e.g., email parsing, expense integration, or supplier direct-connect feeds). **Client acknowledges that Off-Channel Bookings for which itinerary data has not been provided to Direct Travel are not visible** and that Direct Travel cannot locate, alert, or assist Travelers whose travel is not in Direct Travel's systems.

### 3.2 Risk Intelligence and Monitoring

**(a)** Direct Travel will use commercially reasonable efforts to maintain continuous 24/7 monitoring of global events that may impact Travelers, using the Risk Intelligence Platform's intelligence capabilities, which aggregate data from commercially available sources with broad geographic and linguistic coverage.

**(b)** Monitoring includes: event triage and severity classification; identification of potentially impacted Travelers based on available itinerary and location data; initiation of notifications or outreach consistent with Section 5; and layering of active threat alerts on the global risk map for Client visibility.

**(c)** Risk intelligence categories include, without limitation: security threats (terrorism, armed conflict, crime, civil unrest); natural hazards (earthquakes, hurricanes, flooding, wildfire, volcanic activity); health risks (disease outbreaks, pandemic developments, environmental contamination); transportation disruptions (airport closures, airline operational failures, rail strikes, border closures); political and regulatory changes (visa requirement changes, entry restrictions, government advisories); and infrastructure failures (telecommunications outages, power grid failures).

### 3.3 Pre-Trip Services

**(a) Destination Risk Ratings.** Direct Travel will make available to Client and Travelers destination-specific risk ratings sourced from the Risk Intelligence Platform, covering security, health, transportation, and infrastructure dimensions.

**(b) Pre-Trip Advisories.** Where configured in the SOW, Direct Travel will deliver pre-trip briefing materials to Travelers prior to departure, including: destination risk summary; applicable entry requirements (visa, vaccination, testing, documentation); local emergency contact numbers; embassy and consulate locations; and recommended precautions. Briefing content is sourced from the Risk Intelligence Platform and updated on a continuous basis.

**(c) High-Risk Destination Approval.** Where Client has designated specific destinations or risk rating thresholds as requiring enhanced approval, Direct Travel will enforce pre-trip approval workflows within the booking platform, preventing ticketing until the required approval is obtained. Client is responsible for defining the destinations, risk thresholds, and approver hierarchy.

**(d) Travel Insurance Verification.** Where included in the SOW, Direct Travel will verify that Travelers have appropriate travel insurance coverage (including medical treatment, emergency evacuation, and trip disruption) prior to departure. Client is responsible for procuring and maintaining insurance policies.

### 3.4 In-Trip Monitoring and Alerts

**(a) Real-Time Alerts.** Direct Travel will deliver destination-specific or event-based alerts to Travelers through one or more channels (email, SMS, mobile application push notification), as configured in the SOW. Alerts are generated when conditions meeting configured thresholds are detected near a Traveler's current or upcoming destination.

**(b) Alert Content.** Alerts will include: a description of the event or condition; assessed severity; recommended actions where applicable; and Direct Travel emergency contact information. Alerts are **informational** and may include recommended actions; they do not constitute legal, medical, or security advice unless explicitly provided by a contracted Specialist Provider.

**(c) Active Trip Monitoring.** For the duration of a Traveler's trip, Direct Travel will monitor for events that may impact the Traveler's itinerary and, where warranted by severity, initiate proactive outreach consistent with Section 5.

### 3.5 Incident Response and Traveler Assistance

**(a)** During Incidents classified at Severity Level 2 or above, Direct Travel will provide the following services, subject to Severity Level, availability, and supplier constraints:

   - **Welfare checks** — Outreach to potentially impacted Travelers to confirm status (SAFE / NEED ASSISTANCE / NOT IN AREA / NO RESPONSE);
   - **Rebooking and disruption support** — Air, rail, hotel, and ground transportation rebooking, rerouting, and itinerary modification, consistent with travel supplier inventory availability, Client travel policy, and applicable fare rules;
   - **Specialist Provider coordination** — Where Client has contracted Specialist Providers (security, medical, evacuation, insurance), Direct Travel will coordinate referrals and information sharing as authorized by Client;
   - **Client escalation** — Notification to Client's designated contacts per Section 6.

**(b)** The following actions require **express Client authorization** unless otherwise agreed in the SOW:

   - Physical evacuation or extraction from affected areas;
   - Private security deployment or personal protection detail;
   - Medical evacuation or repatriation transport;
   - Charter or non-commercial transportation arrangements;
   - Premium-class rebooking where economy/coach alternatives exist;
   - Any action expected to generate extraordinary costs exceeding the threshold specified in the SOW.

**(c) Immediate Life Safety.** Travelers remain responsible for contacting local emergency services (police, fire, ambulance) where there is an immediate threat to life or safety. Direct Travel services are not a substitute for local emergency response.

### 3.6 Post-Incident Reporting

**(a)** Upon Client request or automatically for Level 4 Incidents, Direct Travel will provide a post-incident summary including: impacted Travelers (where known); timeline of actions taken by Direct Travel; outreach attempts and Traveler response statuses; supplier actions and constraints encountered; and operational observations.

**(b)** Following a Level 4 Incident, Direct Travel will make commercially reasonable efforts to participate in a post-incident review with Client to identify program improvements, assess response effectiveness, and update protocols as warranted.

## 5. SEVERITY LEVELS AND SERVICE LEVEL OBJECTIVES

### 5.1 Severity Level Definitions

| Level | Classification | Description | Examples |
|-------|---------------|-------------|----------|
| **Level 1** | Advisory | Minor disruption with limited direct impact on Travelers; informational alert only | Localized weather warning, minor transportation delay, low-level protest in non-traveler area |
| **Level 2** | Monitor | Moderate disruption that may impact Travelers; targeted alerts issued; optional welfare check | Significant weather event approaching, regional transportation strike announced, elevated security alert in destination city |
| **Level 3** | Action Required | Significant disruption likely impacting one or more Travelers; proactive outreach required | Major weather event (hurricane, earthquake) in Traveler area, civil unrest near Traveler locations, airport closure, disease outbreak with local transmission |
| **Level 4** | Crisis | Immediate life safety risk or large-scale disruption affecting multiple Travelers; urgent outreach and crisis escalation | Terrorist attack, active armed conflict, catastrophic natural disaster, pandemic border closures, mass-casualty transportation incident |

Severity determination is made by Direct Travel using commercially reasonable judgment informed by the Risk Intelligence Platform. Client may request a change to classification at any time; Direct Travel will consider such requests promptly and document the outcome.

### 5.2 Service Level Objectives

The following service level objectives ("**SLOs**") apply from the time Direct Travel determines an Incident requires action under this Exhibit:

| Severity | Traveler Alert / Outreach | Client Escalation Notification | Status Update Cadence |
|----------|--------------------------|-------------------------------|----------------------|
| **Level 1** | Advisory alert within 4 hours | Not required unless Client-configured | As conditions change |
| **Level 2** | Targeted Traveler alert(s) within 2 hours | Notification to primary program contact within 4 hours | Every 4 hours while active |
| **Level 3** | Initiate Traveler outreach within 60 minutes | Notification to escalation roster within 60 minutes | Every 2 hours while active |
| **Level 4** | Initiate Traveler outreach within 30 minutes | Contact Client escalation roster within 30 minutes; Crisis Bridge within 60 minutes | Target: every 30 minutes during acute phase; hourly during sustained phase |

### 5.3 SLO Exceptions

Service level objectives may be impacted by circumstances outside Direct Travel's reasonable control, including without limitation:

- Telecommunications infrastructure outages or degradation in the affected area;
- Government-imposed communication restrictions or internet shutdowns;
- Supplier system outages (GDS, airline, hotel reservation systems);
- Incomplete or inaccurate Traveler profile data (missing mobile number, outdated contact information);
- Off-Channel Bookings for which itinerary data is unavailable;
- Simultaneous multi-region incidents that exceed normal operational capacity;
- Force majeure events as defined in the Agreement.

Direct Travel will document any SLO exception and notify Client of the cause within a reasonable time after the exception is identified.

### 5.4 Traveler Outreach Standards

**(a)** Welfare checks will be attempted via channels configured in the SOW, in the following default priority order unless otherwise specified: (1) SMS, (2) email, (3) mobile application push notification, (4) voice call.

**(b)** Outreach attempts will be logged in the incident activity record, including timestamp, channel used, and outcome.

**(c)** Travelers will be asked to confirm status using a standardized response framework:

| Response | Meaning | TMC Action |
|----------|---------|------------|
| **SAFE** | Traveler is not in immediate danger and does not require assistance | Log status; no further action unless conditions change |
| **NEED ASSISTANCE** | Traveler requires support (rebooking, information, medical/security referral) | Attempt live contact; provide assistance per Section 3.5 |
| **NOT IN AREA** | Traveler is not in or near the affected area | Log status; remove from active outreach for this Incident |
| **No Response** | Traveler has not responded within the configured outreach window | Escalate per Section 6.2 |

**(d)** If a Traveler indicates NEED ASSISTANCE, Direct Travel will attempt live contact (voice call) within 15 minutes and offer assistance consistent with Section 3.5.

## 6. ESCALATION AND COMMUNICATIONS

### 6.1 Client Escalation Roster

Client shall provide Direct Travel with a **24/7 escalation roster** containing, at minimum:

| Role | Required Information | Purpose |
|------|---------------------|---------|
| **Primary Travel Program Owner** | Name, title, phone (mobile), email | First point of contact for all Incidents |
| **Alternate Travel Program Contact** | Name, title, phone (mobile), email | Backup when primary is unavailable |
| **After-Hours Duty Contact** | Name, title, phone (mobile), email | Contact for Incidents occurring outside Client Business Hours |
| **Corporate Security** (if applicable) | Name, title, phone (mobile), email | Contact for security-related Incidents (Level 3–4) |
| **Human Resources Duty Contact** (if applicable) | Name, title, phone (mobile), email | Contact for Incidents involving employee welfare, injury, or fatality |
| **Senior Leadership Contact** | Name, title, phone (mobile), email | Contact for Level 4 Crisis escalation |

Client will keep the roster current and notify Direct Travel of changes within **five (5) business days**. Direct Travel is not responsible for delays or failures in escalation caused by outdated, incorrect, or incomplete roster information.

### 6.2 Non-Responsive Traveler Escalation

For Level 3 and Level 4 Incidents, if a Traveler does not respond to welfare check outreach:

**(a)** Direct Travel will attempt at least **three (3) contacts** across at least **two (2) channels** over a period of **ninety (90) minutes** (or as configured in the SOW).

**(b)** Upon exhaustion of the outreach cadence without response, Direct Travel will escalate the non-responsive Traveler to Client's designated escalation contacts, providing: Traveler name and itinerary details; last known location (based on itinerary data); outreach attempts made and timestamps; and any available supplier-side information (e.g., hotel check-in status, flight boarding status).

**(c)** Further locate attempts (e.g., contacting the hotel front desk, requesting airline manifest confirmation, engaging Specialist Providers for on-ground locate assistance) require **Client authorization** and may incur additional costs.

### 6.3 Crisis Bridge Activation (Level 4)

**(a)** For Level 4 Incidents, where configured in the SOW, Direct Travel will establish a Crisis Bridge within sixty (60) minutes of Level 4 determination.

**(b)** The Crisis Bridge will include, at minimum: Direct Travel's incident coordinator; Client's designated escalation contacts; and, where applicable, Specialist Provider representatives.

**(c)** Direct Travel will use commercially reasonable efforts to provide situation updates ("**SITREPs**") at a target cadence of approximately thirty (30) minutes during the acute phase (first 4 hours) and approximately hourly thereafter, or at an alternative cadence agreed during the incident. Actual cadence may vary based on event dynamics and available information.

**(d)** SITREPs will include: current assessment of event status and trajectory; count and status of identified Travelers (safe, need assistance, non-responsive, not in area, unknown); actions taken and pending; supplier constraints or disruptions affecting response; and recommended next steps.

## 7. TRAVELER DATA, PRIVACY, AND SECURITY

### 7.1 Data Processing

Direct Travel will process Traveler Data only to provide services under the Agreement and as otherwise permitted by the Agreement's data protection terms. Where required by applicable law, a Data Processing Agreement ("**DPA**") is incorporated by reference or attached as a separate exhibit to the Agreement.

### 7.2 Data Controller and Processor Roles

Client is the **data controller** for Traveler Data and is accountable for determining the purposes and means of processing. Direct Travel acts as a **data processor** (or service provider, as applicable under US state privacy laws), processing Traveler Data solely on Client's documented instructions and for the purposes described in this Exhibit and the Agreement.

### 7.3 Lawful Basis for Processing

**(a)** The parties acknowledge that processing of Traveler Data for duty of care purposes is supported by Client's **legitimate interest** in protecting the health and safety of its employees and authorized travelers, as recognized under Article 6(1)(f) of the General Data Protection Regulation ("**GDPR**") and equivalent provisions of applicable data protection laws.

**(b)** Client is responsible for conducting and documenting any required **Legitimate Interest Assessment** or **Data Protection Impact Assessment** relating to traveler tracking, location monitoring, and welfare check communications.

**(c)** Client is responsible for providing appropriate **notice to Travelers** regarding the processing of their personal data for duty of care purposes, including the categories of data collected, the purposes of processing, data recipients, retention periods, and Traveler rights (including the right to object to processing based on legitimate interest).

### 7.4 Minimum Required Traveler Profile Data

Client will use commercially reasonable efforts to ensure Traveler profiles include the following minimum data elements:

| Data Element | Purpose | Impact if Missing |
|-------------|---------|-------------------|
| **Full legal name** (as on government-issued ID) | Traveler identification, supplier matching | Cannot confirm Traveler identity with suppliers |
| **Mobile phone number** (SMS-capable) | Primary welfare check channel | SMS outreach unavailable; degraded response time |
| **Email address** | Secondary alert and communication channel | Email alerts unavailable |
| **Emergency contact** (name, relationship, phone) | Next-of-kin notification in critical situations | Client must provide emergency contact information during incident |
| **Passport details** (number, expiry, issuing country) — where necessary for international travel | International travel documentation verification | Cannot verify travel document validity or assist with consular coordination |
| **Nationality / country of residence** — where necessary for international travel | Consular and immigration assistance | Limited ability to coordinate with appropriate embassy |

Direct Travel's service effectiveness is materially reduced where Traveler profile data is incomplete. Direct Travel is not responsible for inability to contact or assist Travelers whose profile data is missing or inaccurate.

### 7.5 Data Sharing During Incidents

During an Incident, Direct Travel may share the **minimum necessary** Traveler Data with:

**(a)** Client's designated escalation contacts and incident response personnel;

**(b)** Travel suppliers (airlines, hotels, ground transportation providers) to the extent necessary to fulfill rebooking, rerouting, or itinerary changes;

**(c)** Specialist Providers (security, medical, evacuation, insurance), where such providers are specified in the SOW and Client has authorized the sharing;

**(d)** Government authorities or emergency services, where required by law or where Direct Travel reasonably believes disclosure is necessary to protect the life or physical safety of the Traveler.

### 7.6 Location Data and Tracking

**(a)** Traveler location data derived from itinerary records (PNR-based location) reflects the Traveler's **planned** location based on booking data and does not constitute real-time GPS tracking.

**(b)** Where the SOW includes mobile application-based location services, Travelers may be asked to enable location sharing on a voluntary or policy-required basis. Client is responsible for establishing and communicating the policy governing location sharing, including any consent requirements under applicable law.

**(c)** Continuous real-time GPS tracking of Travelers is **not a standard service** and requires explicit inclusion in the SOW, a supporting Data Protection Impact Assessment, and clear Traveler notice. Direct Travel will not implement continuous location tracking without documented Client authorization.

### 7.7 Data Retention

Retention periods for Traveler Data are governed by the Agreement, DPA, and/or SOW. Upon Agreement termination and completion of any transition period, Direct Travel will delete or return Traveler Data in accordance with the Agreement's data protection terms.

### 7.8 Cross-Border Data Transfers

Where Traveler Data is transferred to jurisdictions outside the European Economic Area, United Kingdom, or other jurisdictions with data transfer restrictions, such transfers will be subject to appropriate safeguards as specified in the DPA (e.g., Standard Contractual Clauses, adequacy decisions, binding corporate rules, or other approved transfer mechanisms).

## 9. OFF-CHANNEL BOOKINGS AND VISIBILITY

### 9.1 Visibility Gap

Industry data indicates that approximately 30% of corporate travel bookings are made outside managed travel programs. Direct Travel can only provide Duty of Care Services for travel that is visible in its systems. **Off-Channel Bookings for which itinerary data has not been provided to Direct Travel represent a material gap in Client's duty of care coverage.**

### 9.2 Mitigation Measures

Direct Travel will support the following measures to reduce Off-Channel visibility gaps, where included in the SOW:

**(a)** Integration of supplier direct-connect booking feeds (airline, hotel, car rental);

**(b)** Ingestion of forwarded itinerary confirmation emails via automated email parsing;

**(c)** Expense report data mining for retroactive travel visibility (post-trip only; not suitable for real-time duty of care);

**(d)** Client-configured policy enforcement rules within the booking platform to discourage or restrict Off-Channel bookings.

### 9.3 Client Acknowledgment

Client acknowledges that: (a) Direct Travel has no visibility into, and cannot provide Duty of Care Services for, Off-Channel Bookings unless itinerary data is provided through a supported integration; (b) Travelers who book outside Approved Channels may not receive alerts, welfare checks, or disruption assistance; and (c) Client bears sole responsibility for establishing policies and training to maximize booking compliance.

## 10. LIMITATIONS, DISCLAIMERS, AND ALLOCATION OF RISK

### 10.1 No Guarantee of Safety

Direct Travel does not and cannot guarantee Traveler safety. The Duty of Care Services are designed to support Client's TRM program through information, communication, and coordination. Direct Travel does not control the actions of third parties including airlines, hotels, ground transportation providers, governments, emergency services, telecommunications providers, or other persons or entities.

### 10.2 Dependency on Data and Systems

The effectiveness of Duty of Care Services is dependent on: itinerary visibility (bookings through Approved Channels or supported integrations); Traveler profile data accuracy and completeness; telecommunications infrastructure availability in affected areas; supplier system availability (GDS, airline, hotel reservation systems); and Traveler responsiveness to outreach communications. Direct Travel is not liable for degraded service resulting from deficiencies in these dependencies.

### 10.3 No Professional Advice

Unless expressly stated in the Agreement or SOW, Direct Travel does not provide: medical care, diagnosis, or treatment; legal advice; security operations, armed protection, or physical security services; psychological or mental health services; or immigration or consular services. Where Specialist Providers are included in the SOW, such services are provided by the Specialist Provider under its own terms and professional qualifications, not by Direct Travel.

### 10.4 Supplier and Third-Party Actions

Direct Travel acts as an **agent** in arranging travel services and does not operate airlines, hotels, railways, or ground transportation. Direct Travel is not liable for: the acts, omissions, negligence, or insolvency of travel suppliers; cancellations, delays, overbookings, or service failures by suppliers; conditions at destinations, including political, environmental, health, or safety conditions; or the quality, accuracy, or timeliness of information provided by third-party risk intelligence sources.

### 10.5 Advisory Nature of Risk Intelligence

Risk ratings, destination advisories, and threat assessments are based on commercially available intelligence sources and are provided for informational purposes. They may not reflect real-time conditions and should not be relied upon as the sole basis for travel decisions. Client retains responsibility for making travel authorization decisions.

### 10.6 Package Travel Regulations

Where applicable (e.g., EU Package Travel Directive 2015/2302 or UK Package Travel and Linked Travel Arrangements Regulations 2018), the parties will cooperate to structure arrangements to avoid inadvertent creation of a "package" within the meaning of such regulations, or will allocate responsibilities accordingly if a package is created. ### 10.7 Limitation of Liability

## APPENDIX C — INCIDENT ACTIVITY LOG TEMPLATE

For Level 3 and Level 4 Incidents, Direct Travel will maintain an incident activity log containing the following fields:

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Incident ID** | Unique identifier assigned by Direct Travel |
| **Incident Classification** | Severity Level (1–4) and event type |
| **Date/Time Opened** | UTC timestamp of incident determination |
| **Date/Time Closed** | UTC timestamp of incident resolution or downgrade |
| **Event Description** | Summary of the triggering event |
| **Affected Geography** | Countries, cities, and/or specific locations |
| **Impacted Travelers** | Count and list of identified Travelers in impact zone |
| **Outreach Log** | For each Traveler: timestamp, channel, attempt number, outcome |
| **Traveler Status Summary** | Counts by status: SAFE, NEED ASSISTANCE, NOT IN AREA, No Response |
| **Client Escalation Log** | Timestamps and contacts reached on Client escalation roster |
| **Actions Taken** | Chronological log of TMC actions (rebookings, referrals, coordination) |
| **Specialist Provider Engagement** | Provider name, time engaged, services requested |
| **Crisis Bridge Log** (Level 4) | Activation time, participants, SITREP timestamps |
| **Resolution Notes** | Summary of incident closure and any follow-up actions |
