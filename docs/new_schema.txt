### SeverityLevel — Full Property Definition (Modified)

| Property | Type | Description |
|---|---|---|
| `level` | Integer | 1, 2, 3, or 4 |
| `classification` | Enum | Advisory \| Monitor \| ActionRequired \| Crisis |
| `description` | String | Narrative definition of the tier (§5.1) |
| `alertTimeTarget` | String | SLO for traveler alert/outreach initiation (e.g., "within 30 minutes" for Level 4) |
| `clientEscalationTimeTarget` | String | SLO for client escalation notification (e.g., "within 60 minutes" for Level 3) |
| `statusUpdateCadence` | String | Required frequency of status updates while incident is active (e.g., "every 30 min acute / hourly sustained" for Level 4) |
| `crisisBridgeRequired` | Boolean | Whether a Crisis Bridge must be established (true only for Level 4) |

> **Design rationale:** SLO targets are modeled as properties on SeverityLevel rather than as a separate entity. This keeps the graph concise while still enabling direct queries such as *"What are the time targets for a Level 3 incident?"* without an additional traversal hop. The values map directly to the §5.2 SLO table.

### Obligation — Full Property Definition (New)

| Property | Type | Description |
|---|---|---|
| `description` | String | Plain-language statement of the duty |
| `sectionReference` | String | Document section(s) where the obligation is defined |
| `obligationType` | Enum | Operational \| Data \| Policy \| Financial \| Authorization |
| `obligatedParty` | Enum | TMC \| Client |
| `conditionality` | String | Trigger or condition under which the obligation applies (e.g., "during Level 3–4 Incidents," "for international travel") |

### Alert — Full Property Definition (New)

| Property | Type | Description |
|---|---|---|
| `alertId` | String | Unique identifier |
| `alertType` | Enum | Advisory \| WelfareCheck \| EscalationNotification \| SITREP |
| `channel` | Enum | SMS \| Email \| VoiceCall \| MobileAppPush |
| `timestamp` | DateTime | UTC time of send/attempt |
| `attemptNumber` | Integer | Sequential attempt number for welfare-check retries |
| `content` | String | Message body or template reference (Appendix B) |
| `outcome` | Enum | Delivered \| Failed \| NoResponse \| ResponseReceived |

> **Design rationale:** Outreach channels (SMS, Email, Voice, Push) are modeled as a property on Alert rather than as a separate entity. The channel set is small, stable, and does not carry independent attributes or relationships that would justify entity status.

| `destinations` | List | Cities/countries in the itinerary |
| `captureMethod` | Enum | DirectBooking \| EmailParsing \| SupplierFeed \| ExpenseIntegration |

## Relationship Types

| # | Relationship | Source Entity | Target Entity | Description | Document Basis | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **PARTY_TO** | Organization | Agreement | An organization is a signatory, party, or subject of a contractual document | Direct Travel and Client are parties to the Agreement and Exhibit D (§Preamble, §15) | *Retained* |
| 2 | **INCORPORATES** | Agreement | Agreement | One contractual document is incorporated into, references, or takes precedence over another | Exhibit D is incorporated into the Agreement; SOW defines configurable parameters; DPA incorporated by reference (§1.1, §7.1, §14) | *Retained* |
| 3 | **PROVIDES** | Organization | Service | An organization delivers or performs a duty of care service | Direct Travel provides Itinerary Visibility, Risk Intelligence, Pre-Trip, In-Trip, Incident Response, and Post-Incident services (§3) | *Retained* |
| 4 | **ENGAGES** | Organization | Organization | One organization contracts, subcontracts, or designates another for specialized capabilities | Client engages Specialist Providers; Direct Travel coordinates with Crisis24 for risk intelligence; Client designates medical/security providers (§2.11, §3.5, Appendix A.3) | *Retained* |
| 5 | **OPERATES** | Organization | Platform | An organization manages, hosts, or provides a technology platform | Direct Travel operates the integrated Platform ecosystem; Crisis24 provides the Risk Intelligence Platform (§2.6, §2.14) | *Retained* |
| 6 | **CLASSIFIED_AS** | Incident | SeverityLevel | An incident is assessed and assigned a severity tier that governs response obligations | A terrorist attack is classified Level 4 — Crisis; a localized weather warning is classified Level 1 — Advisory (§5.1) | *Retained* |
| 7 | **CATEGORIZED_AS** | Incident | RiskCategory | An incident is typed under one or more risk intelligence categories | A hurricane is categorized as Natural Hazard; a rail strike is categorized as Transportation Disruption (§2.7, §3.2(c)) | *Retained* |
| 8 | **IMPACTS** | Incident | Traveler | An incident potentially affects one or more travelers who are in or near the impact zone based on itinerary data | Travelers with active itineraries in an earthquake-affected city are identified as impacted (§3.1(c), §5) | *Retained* |
| 9 | **RESPONDS_WITH** | Traveler | TravelerResponseStatus | A traveler replies to a welfare-check outreach with a standardized status | A traveler replies "SAFE" via SMS; another traveler does not respond within the configured window (§5.4(c)) | *Retained* |
| 10 | **ACTIVATED_AT** | Service | SeverityLevel | A duty of care service component is triggered or required at a specific severity threshold | Welfare checks are optional at Level 2, required at Level 3; Crisis Bridge activates at Level 4; post-incident reporting is automatic at Level 4 (§3.5(a), §5.2, §6.3) | *Retained* |
| 11 | **ENABLED_BY** | Service | Platform | A service depends on a technology platform for delivery or execution | Risk Intelligence & Monitoring is enabled by Crisis24 Horizon; Traveler Locate is enabled by the interactive global map and GDS/PNR data (§3.1(b), §3.2(a)) | *Retained* |
| 12 | **COMPLIES_WITH** | Agreement | Regulation | A contractual document or the services it governs are designed to satisfy or align with a regulatory framework | Exhibit D supports Client's compliance with OSHA §5(a)(1), UK HSWA 1974, EU Directive 89/391/EEC, ISO 31030:2021; data processing complies with GDPR (§1.2, §7) | *Retained* |
| 13 | **DESIGNATED_BY** | ContactRole | Organization | An escalation roster role is staffed and maintained by an organization | Client designates the Primary Travel Program Owner, Corporate Security contact, HR Duty Contact, and Senior Leadership Contact (§6.1) | *Retained* |
| 14 | **REQUIRES_DATA** | Service | DataElement | A service depends on specific traveler profile data to function effectively | Welfare checks require mobile phone number (SMS); international travel assistance requires passport details and nationality; emergency escalation requires emergency contact (§7.4) | *Retained* |
| 15 | **TRIGGERS_ACTION** | TravelerResponseStatus | Service | A specific welfare-check response status initiates a defined TMC follow-up action | NEED ASSISTANCE triggers live voice contact within 15 minutes and Incident Response services; No Response triggers non-responsive traveler escalation protocol (§5.4(c)(d), §6.2) | *Retained* |
| 16 | **ESCALATED_TO** | SeverityLevel | ContactRole | A severity level requires notification to specific escalation roster roles, defining the policy-driven escalation path | Level 2 incidents notify Primary Program Contact; Level 3–4 notify Corporate Security and escalation roster within 60 min; Level 4 crises escalate to Senior Leadership within 30 min (§5.2, §6.1, §6.2) | ***Modified*** — *changed from Incident→ContactRole to SeverityLevel→ContactRole to capture policy-driven escalation rules rather than instance-level events* |
| 17 | **HAS_BOOKING** | Traveler | Booking | A traveler has a travel reservation record that provides the TMC with itinerary visibility | Each traveler's PNR contains itinerary segments (air, hotel, rail, car, ground) that enable traveler locate and duty of care services (§2.9, §3.1(a)) | ***New*** — *replaces BOOKS_THROUGH; first half of the Traveler→Booking→BookingChannel chain* |
| 18 | **BOOKED_THROUGH** | Booking | BookingChannel | A booking was made through a specific channel, determining whether the reservation is visible to the TMC for duty of care purposes | Bookings through Approved Channels are captured automatically; Off-Channel Bookings require itinerary capture tools for visibility (§2.1, §2.8, §9.1) | ***New*** — *replaces BOOKS_THROUGH; second half of the chain, enabling queries like "show all bookings with no TMC visibility"* |
| 19 | **ENABLES_COVERAGE** | BookingChannel | Service | An approved booking channel provides the itinerary visibility required for duty of care services to function; off-channel bookings represent a material coverage gap | Approved Channels enable full duty of care coverage; Off-Channel Bookings for which itinerary data has not been provided represent a material gap in duty of care coverage (§3.1(d), §9.1, §9.3) | ***New*** — *models the critical §9 concept that channel choice determines whether services can be rendered* |
| 20 | **REQUIRES_AUTHORIZATION_FROM** | Service | Organization | Specific service actions require express authorization from a designated party before the TMC may proceed | Physical evacuation, private security deployment, medical evacuation, charter transport, premium-class rebooking, and extraordinary costs exceeding SOW threshold all require express Client authorization (§3.5(b)) | ***New*** — *directly models §3.5(b)'s authorization requirements, essential for TMC decision-making* |
| 21 | **ASSIGNED_TO** | Obligation | Organization | A contractual obligation is borne by a specific party (TMC or Client) | Client obligations defined in §8 (escalation roster, travel policy, traveler profiles, insurance, DPIA); TMC obligations defined in §3 (monitoring, outreach, coordination, reporting) and §5 (SLO compliance) | ***New*** — *makes obligations first-class queryable nodes; enables "show all obligations assigned to Client"* |
| 22 | **RELATES_TO** | Obligation | Service | An obligation pertains to, enables, or constrains a specific duty of care service | Client's obligation to maintain complete traveler profiles (§8.4) relates to all services that depend on profile data; Client's obligation to authorize extraordinary measures (§8.6) relates to Incident Response & Traveler Assistance | ***New*** — *links obligations to the services they support or constrain* |
| 23 | **DEFINED_IN** | Obligation | Agreement | An obligation is established or specified within a particular contractual document | TMC outreach obligations defined in Exhibit D §5.4 and §6.2; configurable parameters defined in the SOW (Appendix A); data protection obligations defined in the DPA (§7.1) | ***New*** — *enables traversal from obligation to its contractual source across multiple agreement documents* |
| 24 | **TRIGGERED_BY** | Alert | Incident | An alert or outreach communication is initiated in response to a specific incident | A Level 3 welfare-check SMS is triggered by a hurricane impacting a traveler's destination; a SITREP is triggered by a Level 4 crisis event (§3.4, §5.2, §6.3, Appendix B) | ***New*** — *links communication instances to their causal events for Appendix C activity log compliance* |
| 25 | **SENT_TO** | Alert | Traveler | An alert or welfare-check outreach is delivered to a specific traveler via a configured channel | SMS welfare check sent to traveler's mobile; advisory email sent to traveler's email; voice call attempted to traveler's phone (§5.4, Appendix B, Appendix C) | ***New*** — *enables tracking of outreach attempts, channels, timestamps, and outcomes per Appendix C incident activity log* |
